About Me

Mexican economist pursuing a Master's in Data Science for Public Policy at Hertie School. I have more than six years of experience conducting data-driven research and policy analysis to address violence and impunity in Mexico. I am also the co-founder of Hijas de Internet (Daughters of the Internet), a digital platform which advocates for more inclusive digital spaces.

Projects

Impunity in Intentional Murders and Feminicide, Impunidad Cero 2022

This report analyzes the levels of impunity in intentional homicides and femicides in Mexico. We found that from 2016 to 2021, only 7 out of every 100 intentional homicides resulted in a conviction, and less than half of registered femicides were solved.

Monitoring and Evaluation of Mexico's Criminal Justice System, 2019

This report analyzes the performance of Mexico's criminal justice system in 2019. We highlighted with data the challenges of criminal justice institutions and made recommendations to improve access to justice.

Articles

Mexico’s Violence and Impunity Cycle, Mexico Today 2023

In this article, I examine the cycle of violence and impunity that has plagued Mexico for decades. Only in 2022, 85 people were murdered every single day. Despite the gravity of such crimes, Mexican authorities fail to prosecute them diligently and with a gender perspective.

Artificial intelligence in Security and Justice: Lessons for Mexico

I wrote about the use of Artificial Intelligence in security and justice and its potential risks. In Mexico, we need to start this conversation before AI overtakes us, with technosolutionist perspectives, legal loopholes and without the safeguards to not automate (more) injustices and human rights violations.
